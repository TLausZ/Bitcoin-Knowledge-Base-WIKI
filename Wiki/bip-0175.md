# BIP-0175: Pay to Contract Protocol

## Summary

Utilizing hierarchical deterministic wallets (BIP-32) and the "Purpose Field" (BIP-43), this document specifies the multiparty pay-to-contract key derivation scheme.

### Zweck

Pay-to-Contract (P2C) is a cryptographic technique where a Bitcoin payment address encodes a commitment to an arbitrary contract or document. Two parties agree on a contract hash; the payer tweaks the payee's public key by adding the hash (multiplied by the generator point) before deriving the payment address. The resulting address is spendable by the payee (who knows the tweak) but commits to the contract.

BIP-175 proposed a BIP-32/43 derivation scheme for this. The idea: on-chain payment proves the payer committed to specific contract terms at payment time, without requiring any additional data in the transaction itself.

The proposal was closed — it didn't gain traction, partly because the use cases it addressed (contract-committed payments) were better served by more explicit commitment schemes. The underlying P2C technique itself influenced later proposals including Taproot's key tweaking (BIP-341 uses NUMS key tweaking) and the Pay-to-Contract concept in Lightning channel commitments.
