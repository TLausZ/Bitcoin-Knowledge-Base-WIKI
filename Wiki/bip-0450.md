# BIP-0450: Formosa — Seed encoding by themed mnemonic stories

## Summary

Formosa is an expansion of BIP-39 that maps entropy to short themed sentences (stories) built from syntactically-typed wordlists, substantially improving memorability while retaining BIP-39's cryptographic properties.

### Zweck

BIP-39 encodes seed entropy as a list of 12 or 24 unrelated words ("abandon ability able about...") that most people find difficult to remember reliably. Word lists are random with no semantic connection between them.

Formosa maps each 33 bits of entropy to a short grammatically coherent sentence using typed wordlists (nouns, verbs, adjectives, prepositions). For example: "The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog." Sentences follow a fixed grammar template; the words within each slot are drawn from category-specific lists. The result is a seed phrase that reads as a story — semantically cohesive and far easier to memorise or transcribe without error.

The cryptographic properties are equivalent to BIP-39: the same PBKDF2 derivation to a 512-bit seed. The optional passphrase ("25th word") still applies. As a 2026 Draft, the proposal is very new and lacks wallet adoption.
