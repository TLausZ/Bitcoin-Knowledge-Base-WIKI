# BIP-0337: Compressed Transactions

## Summary

This document proposes a serialization scheme for compressing Bitcoin transactions, achieving less than 50% of original size in many cases.

### Zweck

BIP-0337 defines a compact serialisation format for Bitcoin transactions intended for bandwidth-constrained environments — satellite transmission, steganographic channels, or mesh networks. The format achieves compression by replacing full 32-byte txid + 4-byte vout output point references with shorter block-height/transaction-index references: instead of "txid abc123...", encode "block 840000, tx 17, output 0."

This approach is optionally lossy: the compact references require the receiver to look up the blockchain to reconstruct full txids, which may not always be possible. For standard wallet recovery or backup transmission, this tradeoff is acceptable. For use cases where the receiver might not have blockchain access, the full txid form is retained.

The format was designed for satellite Bitcoin broadcasting (e.g., Blockstream Satellite), where every byte of bandwidth costs money.
