# BIP-0002: BIP process, revised

## Summary

A Bitcoin Improvement Proposal (BIP) is a design document providing information to the Bitcoin community, or describing a new feature for Bitcoin or its processes or environment. The BIP should provide a concise technical specification of the feature and a rationale for the feature. We intend BIPs to be the primary mechanisms for proposing new features, for collecting community input on an issue, and for documenting the design decisions that have gone into Bitcoin. The BIP author is responsible for building consensus within the community and documenting dissenting opinions.

### Zweck

BIP-0002 revised the original BIP-0001 process with several concrete changes: it added explicit licensing requirements (each BIP must declare an open licence), clarified the distinction between Final and Active status, and tightened the criteria for state transitions. It also formalised the role of BIP editors and specified what grounds justify rejecting or withdrawing a proposal.

The revision reflected lessons from the block size debate era, when the informal BIP-1 process proved insufficient to manage contested proposals. BIP-2 tried to make the process more legible and resistant to capture by any single party.

Superseded by BIP-0003 in 2025.
