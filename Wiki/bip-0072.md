# BIP-0072: bitcoin: uri extensions for Payment Protocol

## Summary

This BIP describes an extension to the bitcoin: URI scheme (BIP 21) to support the payment protocol (BIP 70).

## Body

**Typ:** Specification  
**Layer:** Applications  
**Status:** Deployed (aktiviert und im Einsatz)  
**Autor(en):** Gavin Andresen  
**Veröffentlicht:** 2013-07-29  
**Quelle:** https://github.com/bitcoin/bips/blob/master/bip-0072.mediawiki

### Zweck

BIP-0072 adds a `r` parameter to BIP-21 payment URIs that points to a BIP-70 PaymentRequest URL. Example: `bitcoin:1A2B3C?amount=0.01&r=https://merchant.com/pay/invoice123`.

When a wallet that supports BIP-70 scans a BIP-72 URI, it fetches the PaymentRequest from the URL and uses that (with its authentication and richer metadata) instead of the bare address and amount from the URI. Wallets that don't support BIP-70 fall back to the address and amount in the URI — backwards compatible.

The design reflects an awkward transition: BIP-72 is a bridge between the simple BIP-21 world and the richer (but now deprecated) BIP-70 world. With BIP-70 largely abandoned, BIP-72 has little practical relevance today.
