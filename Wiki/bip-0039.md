# BIP-0039: Mnemonic code for generating deterministic keys

## Summary

This BIP describes the implementation of a mnemonic code or mnemonic sentence — a group of easy-to-remember words — for the generation of deterministic wallets.

### Zweck

BIP-0039 defines the "seed phrase" standard — the 12 or 24 words used to back up and restore a Bitcoin wallet. It is one of the most user-facing Bitcoin standards in existence.

Random entropy (128–256 bits) is generated, a checksum appended (the first bits of SHA256 of the entropy), and the combined bits split into 11-bit groups — each mapping to a word from a standardised 2048-word list. 128 bits of entropy plus a 4-bit checksum gives 132 bits, yielding 12 words; 256 bits with an 8-bit checksum gives 264 bits, yielding 24 words.

The mnemonic is not the wallet seed directly. It is passed through PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA512 with 2048 iterations, using the string "mnemonic" + optional passphrase as the salt. The result is a 512-bit seed that feeds BIP-32 HD derivation. The optional passphrase (the "25th word") means two people can hold the same 24 words but protect entirely different wallets — one with a passphrase, one without.

One persistent criticism: BIP-39 mnemonics are not self-describing. The same words produce different wallets depending on the derivation path and passphrase, neither of which is encoded in the words themselves. Recovery requires knowing these parameters out of band.
