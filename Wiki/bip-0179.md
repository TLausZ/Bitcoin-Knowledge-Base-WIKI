# BIP-0179: Name for payment recipient identifiers

## Summary

This BIP proposes renaming "address" to "Bitcoin Invoice Address" for clarity.

## Body

**Typ:** Informational  
**Layer:** unspecified  
**Status:** Draft (noch im Entwurfsstadium)  
**Themen:** bips, lightning
**Autor(en):** Emil Engler, Luke Dashjr  
**Veröffentlicht:** 2019-10-17  
**Quelle:** https://github.com/bitcoin/bips/blob/master/bip-0179.mediawiki

### Zweck

The word "address" is ambiguous and can mislead users into treating Bitcoin addresses like bank account numbers — as stable identifiers to reuse repeatedly. BIP-179 proposes renaming them to "Bitcoin Invoice Address" to emphasise their single-use nature: an invoice address is for one payment, after which a new one should be generated.

The framing echoes how invoices work in traditional finance — you don't give the same invoice number to every customer. By building the "one-time use" concept into the name itself, wallets using the term "invoice address" in their UI would nudge users toward better privacy hygiene without requiring additional explanation.

As a 2019 Draft that hasn't advanced, the proposal illustrates ongoing friction in Bitcoin UX terminology without having achieved adoption.

## Open Questions

- Would renaming "address" to "invoice address" meaningfully change user behaviour around address reuse, or is the problem better addressed in wallet UX without changing the terminology?
