# BIP-0172: Define Bitcoin Subunits as Satoshis

## Summary

This BIP proposes to formally define and standardize Bitcoin's smallest indivisible unit (1/100,000,000 of a bitcoin) as "satoshi" (singular) or "satoshis" (plural), with "sats" as the standard abbreviated form.

### Zweck

BIP-0172 addresses a terminology gap: the word "satoshi" (and its abbreviation "sat") has become standard colloquially but has no formal definition in any BIP. At the protocol level, Bitcoin only counts integer units — what most people call satoshis. The "bitcoin" with its eight decimal places is a presentation convention, not a protocol unit.

This BIP formally establishes that 1 satoshi = 10⁻⁸ BTC, "sat" is the standard abbreviation, and "sats" the plural. The goal is to give wallet developers, exchanges, and standard bodies a citable reference for the terminology. As a 2025 Draft, it's part of a cluster of related denomination proposals (BIP-176 bits, BIP-177 rebase) reflecting ongoing debate about how to present Bitcoin amounts to users as the price per BTC has risen.
