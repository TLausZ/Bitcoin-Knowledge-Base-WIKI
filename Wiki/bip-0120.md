# BIP-0120: Proof of Payment

## Summary

Proof of Payment (PoP) lets you prove that a wallet has the credentials that were used to sign a previously generated transaction — proving that you made a specific payment.

### Zweck

BIP-0120 addressed a specific problem: how do you prove to a third party that you made a Bitcoin payment, without revealing your private key? A payment confirmation from the blockchain tells you the payment was made, but not by whom. A PoP allows the payer to sign a special message that cryptographically demonstrates they control the key that signed the original transaction.

The protocol generates a "proof of payment transaction" — structurally similar to a real transaction but spending the same inputs as the original, to a special OP_RETURN output that includes a nonce provided by the recipient. The recipient can verify the proof without broadcasting it.

Use case: digital goods delivery ("prove you paid invoice #1234 and unlock your download"). The proposal was closed and never broadly adopted. In practice, merchants typically use transaction IDs and block confirmations for proof of payment, or Lightning invoice preimages, which provide cryptographic proof of payment natively.
