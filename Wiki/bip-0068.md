# BIP-0068: Relative lock-time using consensus-enforced sequence numbers

## Summary

This BIP introduces relative lock-time consensus-enforced semantics of the sequence number field to enable a signed transaction input to remain invalid for a defined period of time after confirmation of its corresponding outpoint.

### Zweck

BIP-0068 repurposed the transaction input's `nSequence` field (previously mostly unused after being disabled by Satoshi) to enforce *relative* time locks. Where BIP-65's CLTV enforces an *absolute* time ("cannot spend before block 900000"), BIP-68 enforces a *relative* time ("cannot spend until 10 blocks after this UTXO was confirmed").

The `nSequence` field encodes either a block count or a time-based offset (in 512-second increments) with a flag bit distinguishing the two. If the high bit is not set, the field is interpreted as a relative lock time; if the high bit is set, BIP-68 does not apply (preserving backwards compatibility).

Relative time locks are the foundation of Lightning Network payment channels. When a channel opens, commitment transactions use BIP-68 `nSequence` values to ensure a counterparty can't immediately close and steal funds — the delayed spend gives the other party time to detect cheating and publish a penalty transaction. BIP-112 (OP_CHECKSEQUENCEVERIFY) exposes these relative locks to scripts.
