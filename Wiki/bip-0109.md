# BIP-0109: Two million byte size limit with sigop and sighash limits

## Summary

One-time increase in total amount of transaction data permitted in a block from 1MB to 2MB, with limits on signature operations and hashing.

## Body

**Typ:** Specification  
**Layer:** Consensus (hard fork)

**Themen:** bips
**Autor(en):** Gavin Andresen  
**Veröffentlicht:** 2016-01-28

### Zweck

BIP-0109 was Gavin Andresen's refined block size proposal, more conservative than BIP-101 (which proposed 8MB and doubling every two years). BIP-109 proposed a simple one-time doubling to 2MB, with two additional safeguards that addressed criticisms of BIP-101: explicit limits on signature operations per block (preventing the quadratic hashing attack) and sighash byte limits.

It was implemented in Bitcoin Classic and gained early miner support (around 75% at its peak in early 2016) but the required 75%-of-blocks threshold was never sustained for the required window. The proposal expired and was closed. The sigop and sighash limits it proposed reappeared in BIP-54 (Consensus Cleanup, 2025).
