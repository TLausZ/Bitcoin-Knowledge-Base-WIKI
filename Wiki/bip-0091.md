# BIP-0091: Reduced threshold Segwit MASF

## Summary

This document specifies a method to activate the existing BIP-9 SegWit deployment with a majority hashpower less than 95%.

### Zweck

BIP-0091 was a tactical move during the SegWit activation crisis of 2017. SegWit had been signallable via BIP-9 since late 2016, but some large miners (notably those aligned with the "New York Agreement" / Bitcoin Unlimited camp) were refusing to signal, keeping support below the 95% threshold. Meanwhile, BIP-148 (UASF) was gaining support from users who planned to orphan non-SegWit blocks from August 2017.

BIP-91 proposed that miners signal a new bit (bit 4) with an 80% threshold. If 80% of blocks over a 336-block window signalled bit 4, nodes running BIP-91 would begin rejecting non-SegWit-signalling blocks, effectively forcing SegWit signalling at a lower threshold. This created a bridge: miners could activate SegWit without waiting for the full 95%, and without being forced by BIP-148's UASF deadline.

BIP-91 locked in on July 20, 2017, days before the BIP-148 activation date. SegWit activated on the main network on August 24, 2017. BIP-91 is historically significant as an example of creative soft fork coordination under adversarial conditions.
