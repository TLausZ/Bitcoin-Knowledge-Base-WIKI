# BIP-0077: Async Payjoin

## Summary

BIP-77 defines an asynchronous version of the Payjoin protocol, allowing sender and receiver to coordinate without being online simultaneously.

### Zweck

BIP-0077 (Async Payjoin) addresses the primary usability limitation of BIP-78 (synchronous Payjoin): both sender and receiver must be online at the same time. For most users, especially those running self-hosted stores or cold wallets, this synchrony requirement is impractical.

BIP-77 introduces an asynchronous relay mechanism: the sender deposits an encrypted PSBT with a relay server; the receiver fetches it, adds their inputs, and posts the modified transaction back; the sender polls the relay and broadcasts when ready. The relay only ever sees encrypted PSBTs — it cannot read the transaction contents. The protocol uses end-to-end encryption (ECDH) between sender and receiver keys.

This removes the need for a live connection between the two parties, making Payjoin feasible for wallets that are offline most of the time (mobile wallets, hardware wallets coordinating via companion apps). BIP-77 is still in Draft as of 2025 but is under active implementation by several wallet teams.
