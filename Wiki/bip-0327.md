# BIP-0327: MuSig2 for BIP340-compatible Multi-Signatures

## Summary

This document proposes a standard for the MuSig2 multi-signature scheme, compatible with BIP-340 public keys and signatures. It supports tweaking for BIP-32 key derivation and BIP-341 Taproot outputs.

### Zweck

MuSig2 is a two-round multi-signature scheme for Schnorr signatures (BIP-340). Multiple parties each hold a private key; together they can produce a single aggregate public key and a single aggregate signature indistinguishable from a single-key signature. On-chain, a 3-of-3 MuSig2 setup looks identical to a regular pay-to-pubkey transaction — it reveals nothing about the number of signers.

Compared to its predecessor MuSig1 (three rounds), MuSig2 requires only two rounds of communication: a nonce sharing round and a signature partial-signing round. The nonce can be pre-generated, enabling near-synchronous signing for hardware wallets and other constrained signers.

The security of MuSig2 relies on the signers generating nonces properly — a nonce reuse attack leaks the private key. BIP-327 specifies a deterministic nonce derivation method to mitigate this risk.

MuSig2 enables native on-chain multisig that is cheaper and more private than OP_CHECKMULTISIG. When combined with Taproot key-path spending (BIP-341), a multisig threshold policy pays the same fees as a single-sig transaction. BIP-328 specifies how to derive BIP-32 child keys from a MuSig2 aggregate key.
