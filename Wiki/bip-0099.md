# BIP-0099: Motivation and deployment of consensus rule changes ([soft/hard]forks)

## Summary

This BIP attempts to create a taxonomy of the different types of consensus forks and proposes a deployment mechanism for each of them.

## Body

**Typ:** Informational  
**Layer:** unspecified  
**Status:** Closed (geschlossen (zurückgezogen oder ersetzt))  
**Themen:** bips

**Veröffentlicht:** 2015-06-20

### Zweck

BIP-0099 was an attempt to bring conceptual clarity to the block size debate by categorising consensus changes into a formal taxonomy. It distinguished soft forks (backwards-compatible rule tightenings), hard forks (backwards-incompatible expansions), and "evil forks" (changes that could permanently split the network). It also proposed that soft forks be preferred over hard forks wherever possible, and suggested deployment mechanisms appropriate to each type.

The BIP was closed, likely because the debate it was trying to clarify was moving too fast and too politically for a taxonomy document to be useful. However, the underlying concepts influenced later thinking about fork classification, and the preference for soft forks became the dominant philosophy in Bitcoin development.
