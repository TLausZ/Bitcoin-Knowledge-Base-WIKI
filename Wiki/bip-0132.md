# BIP-0132: Committee-based BIP Acceptance Process

## Summary

This BIP proposes a committee-based process for determining BIP acceptance.

## Body

**Typ:** Process  
**Layer:** unspecified  
**Status:** Closed (geschlossen (zurückgezogen oder ersetzt))  
**Themen:** bips
**Autor(en):** Andy Chase  
**Veröffentlicht:** 2015-08-31  
**Quelle:** https://github.com/bitcoin/bips/blob/master/bip-0132.mediawiki

### Zweck

BIP-0132 proposed replacing the informal BIP acceptance process with a formal committee — a defined group of stakeholders who would vote on whether BIPs advance through stages. The committee would include representatives from miners, users, developers, and businesses.

The proposal reflected frustration during the block size debate when it was unclear who had the authority to "accept" or "reject" a BIP, and different parties claimed different mandates. However, the community broadly rejected the committee approach as inconsistent with Bitcoin's decentralised governance — a committee with formal authority to accept or reject protocol changes would effectively become a governing body for Bitcoin, which was seen as a centralisation risk. BIP-3 (2025) eventually addressed some of the process clarity issues without creating a formal committee.
