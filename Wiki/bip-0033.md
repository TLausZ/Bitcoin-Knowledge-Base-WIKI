# BIP-0033: Stratized Nodes

## Summary

As the Bitcoin network scales, roles are fast becoming specialised. Bitcoin's blockchain becomes more unwieldy for end users over time, negatively affecting the usability of Bitcoin clients.

## Body

**Typ:** Specification  
**Layer:** Peer Services  
**Status:** Closed (geschlossen (zurückgezogen oder ersetzt))  
**Autor(en):** Amir Taaki  
**Veröffentlicht:** 2012-05-15  
**Quelle:** https://github.com/bitcoin/bips/blob/master/bip-0033.mediawiki

### Zweck

BIP-0033 proposed a tiered node architecture where different node types would serve different roles in the network: full nodes with complete blockchain data, "stratized" nodes that could serve as intermediaries for lightweight clients, and thin clients that rely on stratized nodes for data. It aimed to make Bitcoin usable on resource-constrained devices without requiring a full blockchain download.

The proposal was closed without adoption. The same goal was pursued more successfully through BIP-37 (bloom filtering for SPV clients) and later BIP-157/158 (compact block filters), which became the dominant approach for light client support. BIP-33's concept of dedicated intermediary node tiers did not find traction.
