# BIP-0113: Median time-past as endpoint for lock-time calculations

## Summary

This BIP redefines the semantics used in determining a time-locked transaction's eligibility for inclusion in a block. The median of the last 11 blocks is used instead of the block's timestamp.

## Body

**Layer:** Consensus (soft fork)  
**Status:** Deployed (aktiviert und im Einsatz)

### Zweck

Before BIP-113, whether a time-locked transaction (using `nLockTime` with a Unix timestamp) was valid depended on the current block's timestamp. This was exploitable: miners can set their block's timestamp up to two hours in the future and slightly in the past, so a miner could manipulate the timestamp to include or exclude timelocked transactions at will.

BIP-113 replaced the single block timestamp with the Median Time-Past (MTP) — the median of the last 11 block timestamps. MTP increases monotonically (it can never decrease) and is much harder to manipulate: a miner would need to control 6 of the last 11 blocks to shift MTP by one position. This makes timestamp-based timelocks reliable and predictable.

BIP-113 is a prerequisite for sensible timestamp-based timelocks via BIP-65 (CLTV) and BIP-68/112 (CSV with time-based delays). It activated alongside CSV in July 2016.
