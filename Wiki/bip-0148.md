# BIP-0148: Mandatory activation of segwit deployment

## Summary

This document specifies a flag-day activation of the segregated witness BIP-9 deployment, where nodes reject blocks not signalling SegWit support after August 1, 2017.

### Zweck

BIP-0148 is the UASF (User Activated Soft Fork) for SegWit. It specified that starting August 1, 2017, nodes running BIP-148 would reject any block that did not signal readiness for SegWit (BIP-9 bit 1). This was user-enforced activation: miners who refused to signal would have their blocks orphaned by BIP-148 nodes.

The motivation was miner inaction: SegWit had been available for signalling since late 2016, but a coalition of miners (many associated with the "New York Agreement") were withholding support, blocking the 95% threshold. BIP-148 gave users a way to enforce activation without miner consent, by making non-signalling blocks invalid in their view.

The UASF movement gained significant grassroots support in the community. The threat of a chain split was real: if BIP-148 nodes formed a significant portion of economic activity, miners would face a choice between losing revenue on the BIP-148 chain or orphaning their blocks. In the event, BIP-91 achieved SegWit signalling lock-in on July 20, 2017 — before the August 1 BIP-148 deadline — defusing the split risk. SegWit activated on August 24, 2017.

BIP-148 is historically significant as the clearest demonstration that economic nodes (exchanges, wallets, merchants) can exert pressure on miners in contested soft fork activation.
