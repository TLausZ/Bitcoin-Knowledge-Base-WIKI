# BIP-0075: Out of Band Address Exchange using Payment Protocol Encryption

## Summary

This BIP is an extension to BIP 70 that provides two enhancements: it allows the requester to sign the original request, and it encrypts the PaymentRequest to prevent man-in-the-middle viewing.

### Zweck

BIP-0075 extended BIP-70 to enable encrypted, mutually-authenticated payment request exchange. The two additions: senders can optionally sign their payment request (so recipients know who is paying, useful for KYC-compliant payments), and the PaymentRequest can be encrypted end-to-end before being wrapped in TLS — preventing the server or a network observer from reading payment details in transit.

The encryption used ECDH key agreement between the sender's and receiver's ephemeral keys. This was primarily aimed at regulated environments where compliance requires knowing the identity of the payer. It saw limited implementation and became moot when BIP-70 was deprecated.
