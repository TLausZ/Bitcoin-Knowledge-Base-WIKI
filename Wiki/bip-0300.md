# BIP-0300: Hashrate Escrows (Consensus layer)

## Summary

BIP-300 enables a new type of L2 ("drivechain") where withdrawals from the sidechain back to Bitcoin mainchain are governed by miner proof-of-work rather than a federation or fixed set of pubkeys.

## Body

**Typ:** Specification  
**Layer:** Consensus (soft fork)

### Zweck

BIP-0300 (Drivechain) proposes a mechanism for Bitcoin sidechains where BTC can be moved to a sidechain and back without trusting a federation of custodians. The "withdrawal" (sidechain → mainchain) is the hard part: BIP-300 handles it by having miners vote on withdrawal bundles over an extended period (typically 3–6 months). A withdrawal that accumulates enough miner "upvotes" and no "downvotes" becomes valid.

The design intentionally makes withdrawals slow and visible. An attempt by dishonest miners to steal funds would require sustained majority hash power over months — a long window during which the attack is obvious and the community could respond (e.g., via UASF). The security assumption is that miners won't sacrifice their long-term reputation and income for a one-time theft.

The proposal is controversial. Critics argue it gives miners a new avenue to extort sidechain users (by withholding withdrawals) and that miner incentives to steal are underestimated. Proponents argue it allows Bitcoin to gain functionality without consensus on what that functionality should be. BIP-300 has been Draft since 2017 and activation has been proposed and rejected in various contexts.
