# BIP-0001: BIP Purpose and Guidelines

## Summary

We intend BIPs to be the primary mechanisms for proposing new features, for collecting community input on an issue, and for documenting the design decisions that have gone into Bitcoin. The BIP author is responsible for building consensus within the community and documenting dissenting opinions.

### Zweck

BIP-0001 established the BIP system itself, modelled on Python's PEP (Python Enhancement Proposal) process. It defined three BIP types: Standards Track (protocol and consensus changes), Informational (design guidelines or general information), and Process (organisational changes like this one). The lifecycle states it introduced — Draft, Accepted, Rejected, Withdrawn, Final, Deferred — gave the community a shared vocabulary for tracking proposals.

The system was designed so anyone could author a BIP without access to a gatekeeper. Progress required building rough consensus in the community rather than obtaining formal committee approval. This decentralised ownership of the proposal process matched Bitcoin's broader design philosophy.

Superseded first by BIP-0002 and then by BIP-0003, which is the current active process document.
