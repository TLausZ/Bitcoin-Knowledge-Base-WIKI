# BIP-0451: Dust UTXO Disposal Protocol

## Summary

This BIP proposes a protocol for voluntarily disposing of dust UTXOs — outputs too small to spend economically — without requiring a fee that exceeds their value.

### Zweck

Dust attacks (address poisoning) work by sending tiny UTXOs to a target wallet. When the wallet later spends, it may inadvertently include the dust UTXO as an input, linking the dust address to the wallet's other UTXOs and revealing the connection to the attacker.

The problem: dust UTXOs are unspendable in practice — the fee to spend them exceeds their value — but they pollute the wallet's UTXO set and create privacy risks. Standard wallet practice is to leave them unspent forever, where they accumulate in the global UTXO set.

BIP-451 proposes a protocol where multiple parties can collaboratively sweep their dust UTXOs into a single transaction that donates the combined value to miners (as fees). The dust UTXOs are spent; the UTXO set is cleaned; miners collect a small fee reward. No wallet needs to spend more fees than the dust is worth because the costs are aggregated.

As a 2026 Draft, this reflects growing awareness of dust attack prevalence and UTXO set bloat from privacy attacks.

## Open Questions
