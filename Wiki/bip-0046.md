# BIP-0046: Address Scheme for Timelocked Fidelity Bonds

## Summary

This BIP defines the derivation scheme for HD wallets which create timelocked addresses used for creating fidelity bonds.

## Body

**Status:** Draft (noch im Entwurfsstadium)

### Zweck

BIP-0046 defines HD derivation paths for "fidelity bonds" — UTXOs that are timelocked for a period of time. Fidelity bonds are used in the JoinMarket protocol as a Sybil-resistance mechanism: a market maker who has locked bitcoin for a year is harder to impersonate cheaply than one who hasn't, because creating many fake identities would require locking up proportional amounts of capital.

The path structure uses purpose `46'` and creates addresses using `OP_CHECKLOCKTIMEVERIFY` (BIP-65) to enforce the timelock. The BIP also describes how to sign messages with fidelity bond keys for use as certificates — proving ownership of the locked funds without spending them.

As a Draft BIP, it is primarily relevant to JoinMarket participants and privacy-focused users exploring Sybil-resistant coordination mechanisms.
