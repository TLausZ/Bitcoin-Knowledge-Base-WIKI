# BIP-0070: Payment Protocol

## Summary

This BIP describes a protocol for communication between a merchant and their customer, enabling both a better customer experience and better security against man-in-the-middle attacks on the payment process.

## Body

**Typ:** Specification  
**Layer:** Applications  
**Status:** Deployed (aktiviert und im Einsatz)  
**Themen:** bips, lightning
**Autor(en):** Gavin Andresen, Mike Hearn

### Zweck

BIP-0070 replaced simple BIP-21 payment URIs with a full request/response protocol for merchant payments. The flow: a merchant serves a signed PaymentRequest (a Protocol Buffer message) containing the exact output scripts and amounts; the customer's wallet sends a signed Payment message back; the merchant confirms with a PaymentACK.

The design goals were to prevent address substitution attacks (a MITM swapping the destination address) by including a PKI-signed request, and to give merchants richer data about payments (merchant name, transaction memo, expiry time).

BIP-70 was adopted by BitPay, Coinbase, and several wallets in the 2014–2018 era. However it was controversial from the start: it required PKI certificates (centralised CA trust), used Protocol Buffers (complex dependency), and required a live connection between wallet and merchant server. As BIP-21 QR codes proved good enough for most use cases, BIP-70 was deprecated by many implementers. Bitcoin Core removed BIP-70 support in version 0.20 (2020).
