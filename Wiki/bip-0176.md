# BIP-0176: Bits Denomination

## Summary

This BIP proposes "bits" as the standard term for 100 satoshis (1/1,000,000 of a bitcoin).

## Body

**Typ:** Informational  
**Layer:** unspecified  
**Status:** Complete (abgeschlossen)  
**Autor(en):** Jimmy Song  
**Veröffentlicht:** 2017-12-12  
**Quelle:** https://github.com/bitcoin/bips/blob/master/bip-0176.mediawiki

### Zweck

At Bitcoin's current price, expressing amounts in BTC produces awkward numbers like 0.00003500 BTC. Satoshis (3,500 sat) are more readable but the word "satoshi" is long and unfamiliar to newcomers. BIP-176 proposed "bits" as an intermediate denomination: 1 bit = 100 satoshis = 1 µBTC (microbitcoin). At $50,000/BTC, one bit ≈ $0.005.

The denomination fills the gap between BTC (too large for small purchases) and satoshis (not yet mainstream). "Bits" was already in use informally in some wallets and games in 2017; this BIP attempted to formalise it.

The proposal has Status Complete, but "bits" hasn't displaced satoshis in practice — the ecosystem has largely converged on satoshis as the preferred sub-unit, especially as Lightning Network usage grew (where amounts are naturally denominated in sats). BIP-172 and BIP-177 represent more recent takes on the denomination question.
