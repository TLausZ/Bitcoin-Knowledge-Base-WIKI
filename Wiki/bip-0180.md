# BIP-0180: Block size/weight fraud proof

## Summary

A fraud proof that enables light clients to detect oversized or overweight blocks.

## Body

**Typ:** Specification  
**Layer:** Peer Services  
**Status:** Closed (geschlossen (zurückgezogen oder ersetzt))  
**Autor(en):** Luke Dashjr  
**Veröffentlicht:** 2017-03-17  
**Quelle:** https://github.com/bitcoin/bips/blob/master/bip-0180.mediawiki

### Zweck

Light clients (SPV wallets) verify that blocks have valid proof-of-work and contain the correct transaction hashes, but they don't validate block *size* or *weight*. A miner could theoretically produce a block that exceeds the 4 million weight unit limit — full nodes would reject it, but SPV clients might be tricked into accepting it.

BIP-180 proposed a fraud proof mechanism: if a block violates the weight limit, any honest full node could produce a compact proof of this violation that a light client could verify without downloading the full block. The proof would consist of enough of the block's content to demonstrate the weight limit breach.

The proposal was closed. Fraud proofs are technically interesting but difficult to design correctly (you can't prove a block is *too large* without potentially including the excess data), and the light client security model in Bitcoin has generally moved toward the BIP-157/158 approach rather than fraud proofs.
