# BIP-0119: CHECKTEMPLATEVERIFY

## Summary

This BIP proposes OP_CHECKTEMPLATEVERIFY (CTV), a new opcode allowing outputs to commit to the transaction spending them, enabling covenants with predictable spending paths.

## Body

**Typ:** Specification  
**Layer:** Consensus (soft fork)  
**Status:** Draft (noch im Entwurfsstadium)

### Zweck

`OP_CHECKTEMPLATEVERIFY` (CTV) enables covenants in Bitcoin — output scripts that constrain how the coins they contain can be spent. Specifically, CTV checks that the spending transaction matches a committed hash of the transaction's outputs (and some other fields). When you create a CTV output, you predetermine exactly where the funds must go next.

For vaults, a UTXO under CTV can only be spent to a specific recovery script after a specific delay. An attacker with your hot key can trigger a withdrawal, but the funds must pass through a delay transaction first, during which your cold key can reverse it. This is "vaulting" without any new trusted party.

For payment batching, CTV enables congestion control trees: commit bitcoin once to a CTV tree representing many future payments. The on-chain footprint is one small transaction; the individual payments are broadcast when fees are lower or when each recipient needs them. This reduces on-chain pressure during fee spikes without requiring an intermediary.

CTV is deliberately minimal — it only restricts outputs, not inputs. This makes it easier to reason about its security. The criticism is that it's not expressive enough for some use cases, and more general covenant opcodes (OP_CAT, OP_TXHASH) might be preferable. As of 2025, CTV has not been activated.
