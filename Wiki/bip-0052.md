# BIP-0052: Durable, Low Energy Bitcoin PoW

## Summary

This BIP proposes changing Bitcoin's proof-of-work algorithm to reduce energy consumption and enable more accessible mining.

## Body

**Typ:** Specification  
**Layer:** Consensus (hard fork)  
**Status:** Draft (noch im Entwurfsstadium)  
**Autor(en):** Michael Dubrovsky, Bogdan Penkovsky  
**Veröffentlicht:** 2021-05-13  
**Quelle:** https://github.com/bitcoin/bips/blob/master/bip-0052.mediawiki

### Zweck

BIP-0052 proposes replacing Bitcoin's SHA-256d proof-of-work with a new algorithm called "Iltorb" (based on Brotli compression). The authors argue that SHA-256 mining is dominated by ASICs that are designed solely for hashing, consuming electricity as a pure operating expense with no other utility. Their proposed algorithm would instead require significant memory and would be coupled to some "useful" computation.

The proposal would require a hard fork — a backwards-incompatible change to Bitcoin's consensus rules. This is the primary reason it has gone nowhere: hard forks in Bitcoin are considered near-impossible to coordinate without near-universal consensus. Additionally, the Bitcoin community broadly rejects the premise that PoW energy use is a problem to be solved at the protocol level, viewing it as a feature that grounds Bitcoin's security in physical costs.

The BIP remains at Draft status and is widely considered outside the Overton window of changes Bitcoin would adopt.

## Open Questions

- Would any alternative PoW algorithm actually reduce total energy use, or would it just shift consumption to a different hardware class?
