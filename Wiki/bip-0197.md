# BIP-0197: Hashed Time-Locked Collateral Contract

## Summary

This BIP describes a script for a debt agreement contract based on HTLC (BIP-199) transactions. An HTLCC enables cross-chain collateralized loans where Bitcoin serves as collateral for a loan denominated in another blockchain's currency.

### Zweck

BIP-0197 extends HTLC logic (BIP-199) to enable Bitcoin-collateralized loans. The scenario: a borrower locks BTC in an HTLCC script; a lender disburses a stablecoin or other asset on a different chain. If the borrower repays the loan, they recover their BTC. If they default or the collateral value drops below a threshold, the lender can seize the BTC by revealing a preimage (coordinated through the cross-chain protocol).

The script adds a third spending path beyond standard HTLC: a "liquidation" path redeemable by the lender under specific conditions, enabling partially trustless Bitcoin DeFi lending without wrapping BTC on another chain.

As a 2019 Draft, this predates the DeFi explosion. It has not gained broad adoption — most BTC-collateralized lending is done via custodians, not on-chain scripts.
