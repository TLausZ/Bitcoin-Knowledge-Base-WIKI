# BIP-0361: Post Quantum Migration and Legacy Signature Sunset

## Summary

This proposal introduces a pre-announced sunset of legacy ECDSA/Schnorr signatures: Phase A disallows sending funds to quantum-vulnerable addresses; Phase B renders ECDSA/Schnorr spends invalid.

### Zweck

BIP-361 takes a harder stance than BIP-360's migration output: it proposes eventually *invalidating* ECDSA and Schnorr signatures, forcing everyone to migrate to quantum-resistant output types before a set deadline.

Phase A: New UTXOs can no longer be created at quantum-vulnerable address types (P2PK, P2PKH, P2WPKH, P2TR key-path). Users who receive at these addresses can still spend them but must send to quantum-safe outputs. Phase B: Spending from quantum-vulnerable outputs becomes invalid — funds that haven't migrated are frozen.

The proposal addresses a fundamental coordination problem: if quantum computers are developed and only some users migrate, early adopters of post-quantum outputs are safe while laggards remain vulnerable. A sunset creates a forcing function. The obvious controversy: funds that cannot be migrated (lost keys, dormant wallets, Satoshi's coins) would be effectively frozen or confiscated.

As a 2026 Draft, this reflects escalating seriousness about quantum timelines in the Bitcoin community.
