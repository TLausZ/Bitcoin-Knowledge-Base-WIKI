# BIP-0103: Block size following technological growth

## Summary

This BIP proposes a block size growth intended to accommodate hardware and other technological improvements for the foreseeable future.

## Body

**Typ:** Specification  
**Layer:** Consensus (hard fork)  
**Status:** Closed (geschlossen (zurückgezogen oder ersetzt))  
**Themen:** bips
**Autor(en):** Pieter Wuille  
**Veröffentlicht:** 2015-07-21  
**Quelle:** https://github.com/bitcoin/bips/blob/master/bip-0103.mediawiki

### Zweck

BIP-0103 was Pieter Wuille's block size proposal, notable because Wuille was otherwise a strong SegWit advocate rather than a "big block" proponent. It proposed growing the block limit by 17.7% per year — roughly doubling every 4.4 years — tied to observed improvements in bandwidth and storage costs. The rate was calibrated to Moore's Law-adjacent trends rather than to any specific demand projection.

Wuille submitted it to demonstrate that he wasn't categorically opposed to block size increases, but believed any increase should be conservative, predictable, and not rushed. Like the others, it was closed without activation. The SegWit path made it moot.
